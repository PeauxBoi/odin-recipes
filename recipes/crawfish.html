Gumbo is a rich, savory Louisiana stew that blends West African, Native American, and European culinary traditions. It features a dark, flour-and-fat roux, the "holy trinity" of vegetables (celery, bell peppers, and onion), and a variety of meats or seafood. It is traditionally served over steamed white rice

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 pound chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Cook for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Add the sausage and chicken pieces. Cook until browned.
  6. Pour in the chicken broth and diced tomatoes. Add the bay leaves, thyme, and paprika.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
