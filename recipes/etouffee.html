Etouffee, Home


Crawfish Étouffée is a beloved Louisiana Cajun and Creole dish featuring tender crawfish tails smothered in a rich, spiced, roux-based sauce, traditionally served over white rice. The word étouffée is French for "smothered" or "suffocated," which perfectly describes how the shellfish is cooked down into a flavorful gravy.

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • all-purpose flour
  • Vegetables
  • Cajun Seasonings
  • Cayenne Pepper
  • Salt and Black pepper
  • Stock
  • Crawfish Tails
  • Fresh Chopped Parsley
  • Cooked Rice

Steps

  1. Add butter and flour
  2. Cook the roux until it is a dark brown color
  3. Add the vegetables and cook until they are soft
  4. Add the seasonings and cook for a few minutes
  5. Add the stock and bring to a simmer
  6. Add the crawfish tails and cook until they are heated through
  7. Serve over cooked rice and garnish with fresh parsley
